“Suspending Damage”

111111

Eve Tuck 2009 · 5 min. read

Exam-oriented education reflects a damage-centered logic — it sees students as deficient and in need of fixing.

Tuck describes damage-centered research as one that “frames entire communities as depleted, ruined, and hopeless” (p. 409).

She warns: “All we are left with is the damage” — this mirrors how exam-based systems reduce students to scores (p. 415).

In contrast, desire-based education values “hope, visions, and the wisdom of lived lives” (p. 417), empowering youth to imagine and build futures.
